Who am I?

It has now been over a decade since I started learning programming. I am now sharing with you some of the fruits of that work. I am an ordinary person, but I share your thirst for knowledge. My field is video games — one of the least forgiving, but the most fun. I have a number of prototypes under my belt, along with several certifications (Udemy, Unity, Harvard). But that matters less in a field that has been deeply shaken by the rise of AI in recent years.

The Method

Mental fitness for code — consistency before talent.

The MadHackAdemy method rests on a simple principle: you don't learn to code by watching — you learn by repeating. Like at the gym — it's not the heroic session that makes the difference, it's consistency. A little every day, even when it's not glamorous.

FlashDev keeps you coming back, card after card, exercise after exercise. At first, you reproduce patterns without always grasping everything — and that's normal. You train your brain like a muscle: the motion first, fluency later.

Then, if you follow the protocol, something shifts. The code you copy mechanically becomes code you understand. Repetition doesn't replace thinking — it prepares it. That's mental fitness: train until it becomes natural, then let understanding emerge on its own.
